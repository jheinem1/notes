Polynomial Approximation

To evaulate a polynomial (such as f(x)=x8−4x5+12f(x)=x^8-4x^5+\frac12f(x)=x8−4x5+21​), all we need is arithmetic. However, algebraic function such as f(x)=x4−13f(x)=\sqrt[3]{x^4-1}f(x)=3x4−1​ and the trigonometric, logarithmic, and exponential functions cannot be evaluated with arithmetic. Because of this, it makes sense to approximate these functions.

Taylor Polynomials are a good way to do this.
