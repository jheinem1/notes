Work

The change in energy when a force causes a displacement of an object. For example, carrying a refrigerator up a flight of stairs.

If a constant force FFF displaces an object a distance ddd in the direction of the force, the work done is work=force∗distance\text{work}=\text{force}*\text{distance}work=force∗distance.

Calculus deals with variable forces:

Definition

The work done by a variable force FFF in moving an object along a line from x=ax=ax=a to x=bx=bx=b in the direction of the force is

W=∫abF(x)dx.W=\int_a^b{F(x)}{dx}\text.W=∫ab​F(x)dx.
