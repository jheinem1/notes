Repeated Linear Factors

A simple factor is raised to the first power, however a repeated factor is raised to a power higher than the first power.

Suppose (x−r)m(x-r)^m(x−r)m appears in the denominator, where m>1m>1m>1 is an integer. Then there must be a partial fraction for each power of (x−r)(x-r)(x−r) up to and including the mmmth power. For example, if x2(x−3)4x^2(x-3)^4x2(x−3)4 appears in the denominator, then the partial fraction decomposition includes the 6 terms

The rest of the procedure remains the same, though the amount of work increases with the number of coefficients.

Procedure

Suppose the repeated linear factor (x−r)m(x-r)^m(x−r)m appears in the denominator of a proper rational function in reduced form.

The partial fraction decomposition has a partial fraction for each power of (x−r)(x-r)(x−r) up to and including the mmmth power; that is, the partial fraction decomposition contains the sum
