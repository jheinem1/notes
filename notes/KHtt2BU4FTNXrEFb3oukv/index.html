Arithmetic properties

Distributive/Factoring property

Fraction properties

  • Fractions added or subtracted need to share the same deoniminator
  • Multiplication between fractions is straight across
  • Division can be done by multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second (flip it)
  • Can be broken up by numerator, but not denominator

Fraction in denominator of fraction

Exponent properties

  • Multiplying two exponents with the same base add the exponents
    • ab+ac=ab+ca^b+a^c=a^{b+c}ab+ac=ab+c
  • An exponent to one degree raised to another multiplies the exponents
    • (an)x=an∗x(a^n)^x=a^{n*x}(an)x=an∗x
  • Group of numbers raised to the same power can be separated
    • (ab)x=axbx(ab)^x=a^xb^x(ab)x=axbx
  • Negative exponent can/should be moved to the denominator (or numerator)
    • a−m=1ama^{-m}=\frac{1}{a^m}a−m=am1​
    • 1a−m=am\frac{1}{a^{-m}}=a^ma−m1​=am
  • Dividing exponents means subtraction
    • anam=an−m\frac{a^n}{a^m}=a^{n-m}aman​=an−m
  • Fractions raised to a power can be split up
    • (ab)n=anbn(\frac{a}{b})^n=\frac{a^n}{b^n}(ba​)n=bnan​
  • Fractions raised to a negative power can be flipped
    • 12−1=21\frac{1}{2}^{-1}=\frac{2}{1}21​−1=12​
  • Anything to the first degree equals itself
    • x1=xx^1=xx1=x
  • Anything to the 0 degree equals 1
    • x0=1x^0=1x0=1

Radical properties

  • The nnnth root of any number is equal to the number to the power of 1 over the root
  • The nnnth root of aaa times bbb can be split into the nnnth root of aaa times the nnnth root of bbb
  • The nth root of aaa over bbb is equal to the nnnth root of aaa over the nnnth root of bbb
  • The nnnth root of aaa to the power of mmm is equal to aaa to the power of mmm over nnn
  • The nnnth root of aaa to the nnn power equal aaa if nnn is odd
  • The nnnth root of aaa to the nnn power is the absolute value of aaa or plus or minus aaa
